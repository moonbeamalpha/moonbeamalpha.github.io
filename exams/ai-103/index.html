The exam

What is the AI-103 exam?

AI-103 is the exam for the Microsoft Certified: Azure AI Apps and Agents Developer Associate credential. It is aimed at Azure AI engineers who build, manage, and deploy agents and AI solutions with Microsoft Foundry. The exam expects practical Python experience and familiarity with general AI, generative AI, and Azure services.

AI-103 is hands-on and SDK-aware. It validates that you can plan and manage Azure AI solutions; implement generative AI and agentic solutions; build computer vision solutions; implement text analysis and speech workflows; and create information-extraction and retrieval pipelines. Expect scenarios involving Python, Foundry projects, models, agents, Azure AI Search, Content Understanding, security, evaluation, and observability.

AI-103 exam objectives

Five domains from Microsoft's current outline. Every summary below is paraphrased from the official study guide; Azure Mastery tags each question to its assessed domain so you can see where your weak spots cluster.

Plan and manage an Azure AI solution25–30%

Choose appropriate Foundry services, models, retrieval methods, and agent integrations. Set up projects and deployments, connect CI/CD, manage quotas and cost, monitor quality and safety, configure managed identity and private networking, and apply responsible-AI controls.

Implement generative AI and agentic solutions30–35%

Build generative apps and agents with Foundry, RAG, SDKs and connectors. Define tools, memory and conversation handling, orchestrate multiple agents, add safeguards and approval flows, evaluate quality and safety, and operationalise workloads with tracing, token analytics, and latency signals.

Implement computer vision solutions10–15%

Generate and edit images and video, build multimodal understanding workflows, produce captions and accessible descriptions, analyse visual and video evidence with Content Understanding, and implement safety controls for multimodal content.

Implement text analysis solutions10–15%

Extract entities, topics, summaries, and structured output; detect sentiment, tone, safety issues, and sensitive content; translate text; and implement speech-to-text, text-to-speech, custom speech, audio reasoning, and speech translation workflows.

Implement information extraction solutions10–15%

Ingest and index documents, images, audio, and video for grounding; configure semantic, hybrid, and vector search; enrich content with built-in or custom skills; build OCR-based RAG ingestion; and use Content Understanding to extract structured or Markdown outputs.

AI-103 FAQs

How much does the AI-103 exam cost?

The AI-103 voucher is USD $165 in the United States. Pricing varies by region — in the UK it's typically around £128. Microsoft sometimes runs free-voucher promotions during events such as Microsoft Build or Microsoft Ignite, so check your Microsoft Learn profile for any active offers before booking. AI-103 also requires annual renewal (free, online), so factor that into long-term cost planning.

Does the AI-103 certification expire?

Yes. Microsoft Associate certifications including AI-103 expire annually. Renewal is free — a 25–30 question online assessment on Microsoft Learn within the six-month window before your expiration date. The renewal targets recent skills outline updates, so staying current is straightforward if you remain broadly active in the role. (Fundamentals certifications such as AZ-900 are different — those don't expire.)

What is the AI-103 retake policy if I fail?

The first retake is allowed after 24 hours. Second and third retakes each require a 14-day wait. Microsoft caps retakes at five attempts per 12-month rolling period. Each attempt requires a new voucher purchase.

How long should I study for AI-103?

Allow several weeks of focused study and hands-on practice. AI-103 expects experience developing apps with Python plus familiarity with Microsoft Foundry, general AI, generative AI, and Azure services. Azure Mastery's readiness gauge helps you decide when you're ready to schedule the exam.

AI-103 vs AI-102 — different roles?

AI-102 retired on 30 June 2026. AI-103 is the current exam for the Azure AI Apps and Agents Developer Associate credential. Its five domains cover planning and managing Azure AI solutions, generative and agentic solutions, computer vision, text analysis, and information extraction.

AI-103 vs AI-900 — which should I take first?

AI-900 first if AI concepts are new to you. AI-900 (Microsoft Azure AI Fundamentals) teaches the AI vocabulary and the responsible-AI principles without expecting code. AI-103 is the role-based Associate exam: it expects hands-on Python or .NET SDK work against Azure OpenAI and Azure AI Foundry. Most candidates pass AI-900 in a few weeks then spend two to three months on AI-103.
