The exam

What is the AB-100 exam?

AB-100 is the Microsoft Agentic AI Business Solutions Architect credential — the senior-architect exam for designing, deploying, and governing agentic-first business solutions across the full Microsoft AI stack: Copilot Studio, Microsoft Foundry, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Dynamics 365 Copilot, and Power Platform AI. AB-100 covers planning AI-powered business solutions (ROI analysis, multi-agent strategy), designing agents and orchestration (task / autonomous / prompt-and-response agents, MCP extensibility, Foundry Tools), and deploying with full ALM, telemetry, responsible AI, and security.

AB-100 is breadth-heavy and architecture-first. It validates that you can analyse business requirements and recommend agentic patterns, decide build-vs-buy across Foundry / Copilot Studio / prebuilt agents, design multi-agent solutions using Agent2Agent (A2A) and Model Context Protocol (MCP), orchestrate Microsoft 365 Copilot for Sales / Service and Dynamics 365 AI, and define ALM, governance, and audit trails for agents and AI models. Expect scenario questions that hand you a multi-system enterprise architecture and ask you to recommend the right primitive, the right deployment slot, or the right control.

Microsoft published the AB-100 skills outline in May 2026. Every question in Azure Mastery's AB-100 bank is mapped to the current outline — including the new MCP extensibility, Computer Use, agent feed, and DSPM-for-AI topics. Read the official outline at learn.microsoft.com.

10-week study plan

Suggested AB-100 study plan

Most candidates pass AB-100 after eight to twelve weeks of focused study, depending on hands-on experience with Copilot Studio, Microsoft Foundry, and Dynamics 365 Copilot. The ten-week plan below maps onto the three AB-100 domains (weighted toward Deploy at 40–45%), Azure Mastery's adaptive sessions, and the in-app exam simulator. Adjust pace to taste — the readiness gauge tells you when you're done, not the calendar.

  1. Plan AI-powered business solutions

    • Week 1: Requirements analysis — agent use cases for task automation, data analytics, and decision-making; grounding-data review (accuracy, relevance, timeliness); organising business data for AI consumption.
    • Week 2: Overall AI strategy — Cloud Adoption Framework for Azure (AI track), multi-agent solutions across Microsoft 365 Copilot / Copilot Studio / Microsoft Foundry, build-vs-buy-vs-extend, prompt-engineering guidelines, AI Center of Excellence.
    • Week 3: ROI analysis — total cost of ownership, build/buy/extend trade-offs, Dynamics 365 multi-app AI scenarios.

  2. Design AI-powered business solutions

    • Week 4: Design agents and AI — Dynamics 365 customer experience/service Copilot customisations, task / autonomous / prompt-and-response agents, Foundry Tools, topics with fallback in Copilot Studio.
    • Week 5: Extensibility — custom models in Foundry, agents in Microsoft 365 Copilot, MCP-based extensibility, Computer Use, agent behaviours including reasoning and voice mode.
    • Week 6: Orchestrate prebuilt agents — Dynamics 365 finance/supply chain, customer experience/service, Microsoft 365 Copilot for Sales / Service, Power Platform AI Hub, model routing.

  3. Deploy AI-powered business solutions

    • Week 7: Analyse, monitor, and tune — agent-monitoring tools, backlog and user-feedback analysis, telemetry interpretation, model-performance metrics.
    • Week 8: Testing — agent test strategy and metrics, validation criteria for custom AI models, end-to-end scenarios across multiple Dynamics 365 apps, prompt best-practices validation.
    • Week 9: ALM, responsible AI, security, governance — ALM for data / Copilot Studio agents / Foundry agents / custom AI models / Dynamics 365 AI; agent and model security; prompt-manipulation mitigations; responsible-AI review; data-residency compliance; access controls; audit trails.

  4. Sharpen, simulate, schedule

    • Week 10: Run Focus Weak Spots every morning, then two end-to-end Exam Simulator runs at full 100-minute length. Schedule the exam when readiness gauge is 740+ with reasonable confidence — AB-100 punishes a single weak domain.

AB-100 FAQs

How much does the AB-100 exam cost?

The AB-100 voucher is USD $165 in the United States. Pricing varies by region — in the UK it's typically around £128. AB-100 is a senior architecture exam, so Microsoft rarely discounts it in free-voucher promotions; if a voucher comes up via Microsoft Build or Microsoft Ignite, grab it. The credential also requires annual renewal (free, online), so factor that into long-term cost planning.

Does the AB-100 certification expire?

Yes. Microsoft specialty and associate certifications including AB-100 expire annually. Renewal is free — a 25–30 question online assessment on Microsoft Learn within the six-month window before your expiration date. The renewal targets recent skills outline updates (Foundry models, Copilot Studio MCP features, Dynamics 365 Copilot orchestration), so staying current is straightforward if you remain broadly active in agentic AI architecture work. (Fundamentals certifications such as AB-900 are different — those don't expire.)

What is the AB-100 retake policy if I fail?

The first retake is allowed after 24 hours. Second and third retakes each require a 14-day wait. Microsoft caps retakes at five attempts per 12-month rolling period. Each attempt requires a new voucher purchase. AB-100 has a low public pass-rate among candidates because it spans the entire Microsoft AI surface (Foundry, Copilot Studio, Dynamics 365 Copilot, MCP, A2A) — invest in the readiness gauge rather than rebooking blindly.

How long should I study for AB-100?

Most candidates pass AB-100 after eight to twelve weeks of focused study, assuming hands-on experience with at least one of Copilot Studio, Foundry, or Dynamics 365 Copilot. If multi-agent orchestration is genuinely new to you, plan for three to four months — the exam expects you to design end-to-end agentic-first solutions, not just describe concepts. Azure Mastery's readiness gauge tells you when you're at exam-ready; don't book until it shows roughly 740 or higher with reasonable confidence.

AB-100 vs AB-620 — which agent cert should I take?

Different roles. AB-620 (AI Agent Builder) is the maker-level cert focused on building agents in Copilot Studio — topics, generative answers, knowledge sources, channels. AB-100 (Agentic AI Business Solutions Architect) sits above it: it expects you to architect multi-agent, cross-platform solutions that span Copilot Studio, Microsoft Foundry, Dynamics 365 apps, and Power Platform — plus ALM, governance, ROI analysis, responsible AI, and security. AB-620 first if you're building agents day-to-day; AB-100 if you're leading the architecture decisions across teams.

AB-100 vs AI-102 vs AI-103 — how does it fit with the AI engineer track?

AI-102 and AI-103 are code-first developer / engineer certs (Python / .NET, Azure AI Foundry SDK, agent code). AB-100 is the architect-level cert that spans the entire Microsoft AI surface — including Foundry, but also Copilot Studio, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Dynamics 365 Copilot, prebuilt agents, and Power Platform AI. If you write code, do AI-103 first then AB-100 to round out your architecture skills. If you lead enterprise agentic AI strategy, AB-100 is the headline credential.
